# ECONOMICS.md

## Assumptions

Because Credex brokers discounted AI infrastructure credits, the economic value of a converted lead depends on the size of the customer's annual AI spend and the margin Credex earns on each transaction.

For this analysis, I use the following assumptions:

- Average customer annual AI spend routed through Credex: $24,000
- Average discount delivered to the customer: 20%
- Credex gross margin on each deal: 10%
- Gross profit per converted customer: $2,400
- Customer retention: 12 months minimum

These are rough assumptions, but they provide a realistic framework for evaluating the tool.

---

## What a Converted Lead Is Worth

If one customer routes $24,000 of annual AI spend through Credex and Credex earns a 10% margin:

Gross Profit = $24,000 × 10% = $2,400

Therefore, a single converted customer is worth approximately **$2,400 in gross profit**.

---

## Conversion Funnel Assumptions

Starting with 100 completed audits:

1. 30% submit their email → 30 leads
2. 20% of leads book a consultation → 6 consultations
3. 25% of consultations purchase credits → 1.5 customers

Expected gross profit:

1.5 × $2,400 = **$3,600**

Value per completed audit:

$3,600 ÷ 100 = **$36 per audit**

---

## Customer Acquisition Cost by Channel

### Product Hunt / Hacker News
- Posting cost: $0
- Time cost only
- Effective CAC: near $0 if the launch gains traction

### Founder Outreach
- 50 personalized messages
- 5 responses
- 2 completed audits
- 0.2 customers
- Time-intensive but still low cash cost

### Credex-Owned Traffic
- Existing website traffic and outbound sales
- Marginal CAC close to zero

### Content Marketing
- Requires upfront time investment
- Scales well if articles rank for high-intent keywords

For an early-stage estimate, I assume blended CAC of **$100 per converted customer**.

---

## Profitability Threshold

With $2,400 gross profit per customer and $100 CAC:

Contribution Profit = $2,400 − $100 = **$2,300**

This leaves a large margin of safety.

Even if conversion rates are materially lower, the economics remain attractive.

---

## Minimum Conversion Rates Needed

Let:
- Lead-to-customer conversion = 5%
- Value per customer = $2,400

Expected value per lead:

0.05 × $2,400 = **$120**

If the cost to acquire a lead is below $120, the funnel is profitable.

---

## Audit-to-Customer Conversion Needed

If each audit costs approximately $1 in infrastructure and operations, then:

Required conversion rate = $1 ÷ $2,400 = **0.042%**

In practice, even very modest conversion rates make the tool economically viable.

---

## Path to $1M ARR in 18 Months

To generate $1,000,000 in annual gross profit:

Required customers:

$1,000,000 ÷ $2,400 ≈ **417 customers**

Monthly customer target over 18 months:

417 ÷ 18 ≈ **23 new customers per month**

Using the earlier funnel assumptions:

- 100 audits → 1.5 customers
- To acquire 23 customers/month:
  - 23 ÷ 1.5 ≈ 16 funnel units
  - 16 × 100 = **1,600 completed audits per month**

This equals roughly:

- 53 completed audits per day

This is achievable if StackTrim becomes a trusted benchmark tool within founder and engineering communities.

---

## Sensitivity Analysis

### Conservative Case
- Gross profit per customer: $1,000
- Audit-to-customer conversion: 0.5%

Still profitable, but requires higher audit volume.

### Upside Case
- Gross profit per customer: $5,000+
- Strong conversion from high-savings audits

The tool becomes highly leveraged because a single enterprise customer can justify thousands of audits.

---

## Why This Tool Is Economically Attractive

StackTrim has favorable economics because:

1. It solves a clear pain point.
2. It delivers value before collecting contact information.
3. Distribution can be largely organic.
4. Credex monetizes high-value customers rather than charging users directly.
5. The cost to run each audit is extremely low.

Even modest conversion rates create a strong return on investment.

---

## Conclusion

Under reasonable assumptions, each completed audit is worth approximately **$36** in expected gross profit to Credex, while infrastructure costs are negligible.

That makes StackTrim a compelling lead-generation product: users receive immediate value, and Credex acquires qualified, high-intent prospects at very low cost.
