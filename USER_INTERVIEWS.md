# USER_INTERVIEWS.md

I was not able to complete the three required user interviews within the assignment window.

This was a gap in my process, and if I were doing this again, user interviews would be one of the first activities I completed rather than something I planned to do near the end.

## Why I Missed This Requirement

I underestimated the total scope of the assignment, particularly the amount of time required for deployment, debugging, CI setup, and the extensive written deliverables. I prioritized building a fully functional end-to-end product and left user interviews too late in the process.

That was a mistake. The assignment made it clear that talking to real users was a non-negotiable part of the evaluation.

## How I Would Conduct the Interviews

My plan was to speak with:

1. A startup founder using ChatGPT, Claude, and Cursor.
2. An engineering manager responsible for developer tooling budgets.
3. An indie hacker managing AI expenses personally.

Key questions I intended to ask:

- How do you currently track AI tool spending?
- Have you ever discovered overlapping subscriptions?
- What level of projected savings would motivate you to take action?
- Would you share this report internally?
- What concerns would prevent you from submitting your email?

## Assumptions I Made in the Absence of Interviews

Without direct user conversations, I made several assumptions:

- Founders care most about total monthly and annual savings.
- Users are more willing to provide contact details after seeing clear value.
- Shareable reports increase the likelihood of internal discussion.
- Savings above $500/month are meaningful enough to justify a consultation.

These assumptions shaped the product, but they remain hypotheses that should be validated with real users.

## What I Would Validate First

If I continued working on StackTrim, the first priority would be conducting user interviews to validate:

- Whether the audit recommendations are perceived as credible.
- Which parts of the report are most persuasive.
- Whether the email gate appears at the right moment.
- What additional data users would want before contacting Credex.

## Reflection

Not completing the interviews was the most significant weakness in my submission. Building the product was valuable, but real conversations would have improved both the positioning and the audit experience. This is the first area I would address in a second iteration.
